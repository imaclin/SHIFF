3 Ways to Raise the Stakes

All three could coexist — or you could go deep on one. Pick what resonates.

A

The Numbers Don't Lie — Impact Stats Section

A stark, full-width section with 4–5 massive numbers that land like gut punches before visitors even scroll to the characters. Think: "0 player suspensions. 30+ years in federal prison for the fans." The asymmetry of that injustice IS the show.

111 — Federal counts against Dale Shiffman
20+ — MLB players who testified
0 — Player suspensions handed down
7 — Fans who went to federal prison
B

The Betrayal — A Dedicated "The Wire" Moment

Right now the betrayal (Kevin wearing a wire against his best friend) is buried in character bios. It deserves its own cinematic section. A full-bleed dark panel, a single quote, and the bare facts of what happened — designed to stop the reader cold and make them feel the weight of that choice.

"The worst day of Dale's life was his arrest.
The next day was worse."
C

The Timeline — Rise and Fall as a Visual Arc

A scrollable timeline from 1979 (championship) to 1985 (trial verdict) that shows the escalation. Each milestone is a scene — the Parrot's debut, the first gram, the Miami deal, Rod Scurry collapsing in the bullpen, the FBI investigation, the wire, the arrest, the trial. Readers feel the inevitability of the crash.

1979 → World Champions → 1980 → First deals → 1983 → Miami → 1984 → FBI → 1985 → Trial
